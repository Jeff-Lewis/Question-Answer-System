In the wake of improving economic data, including today's strong personal income and spending report, Deutsche Bank's Chief U.S. Economist Joe LaVorgna tweeted that the economy may be growing at a 4 percent rate. Many economists like PIMCO's Mohamed El-Erian and ECRI's Lakshman Achuthan have argued that the post-financial crisis U.S. economy would grow at below trend, which is around 3 percent, for years.
