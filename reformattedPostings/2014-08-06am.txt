Thomson ReutersTwenty-First Century Fox Inc CEO Murdoch smiles on the second day of the Allen and Co. media conference in Sun Valley, Idaho(Reuters) - Twenty-First Century Fox Inc &lt;foxa.o&gt;, the film and TV company controlled by Rupert Murdoch, reported a 16.8 percent rise in quarterly revenue, helped by the box office success of films such as "X-Men: Days of Future Past" and growth in its cable network business. The company, separated from News Corp &lt;nwsa.o&gt; last June, said revenue rose to $8.42 billion in the fourth quarter ended June 30, from $7.2 billion a year earlier. Net income attributable to shareholders was $999 million, or 45 cents per share, compared with a loss of $371 million, or 16 cents per share in the same quarter of 2013. The company withdrew its $80 billion offer to buy Time Warner Inc &lt;twx.n&gt; on Tuesday, abandoning plans to create one of the world's largest media conglomerates.     (Reporting by Abhirup Roy in Bangalore; Editing by Ted Kerr) FREE AppDownload
