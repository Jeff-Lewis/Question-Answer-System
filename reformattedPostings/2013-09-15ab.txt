REUTERS/Jason ReedThis afternoon, Larry Summers announced he was withdrawing his name for Fed Chair. Now, the Wall Street Journal's Damian Paletta reports Treasury Secretary Tim Geithner, who'd previously expressed reluctance to take the position, is holding firm and will not seek the nomination. That leaves current Vice Chair Janet Yellen and Yellen's immediate predecessor, Don Kohn, as the lone, known candidates. We've profiled both — Yellen remains the favorite thanks to her position supporting current policies, and has the backing of 200+ academics. Kohn, a lifelong Fed official, would likely face a smoother confirmation hearing.
