michael_swan via Compfight ccThe Federal Reserve just released U.S. consumer credit data for the month of August. Total credit expanded $13.625 billion (or 5.4%) in August to $3.037 trillion. Economists expected a smaller, $12.0 billion expansion after an upward-revised $10.4 billion advance in July. Revolving credit — credit cards and the like — fell $0.9 billion (1.2%) to $848.9 billion.
