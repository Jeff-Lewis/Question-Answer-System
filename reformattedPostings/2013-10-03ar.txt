ISM's monthly gauge of the American services sector came in well below expectations, and stocks have been falling while bonds have been rising since the 10 AM release. The S&amp;P 500 is now down 1.4%, trading around 1670, while 10-year U.S. Treasury futures are up 0.4%, and the yield on the 10-year Treasury note is three basis points lower at 2.58%. The headline index fell to 54.4 in September from August's 58.6 reading, missing economists' estimates for a smaller drop to 57.0 and indicating a significant slowdown in the pace of growth of the American services sector in September. The employment sub-index dropped to 52.7 from 57.0. In a week where the release of the monthly jobs report is postponed due to a government shutdown, the number stands out.
