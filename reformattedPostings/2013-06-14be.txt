The preliminary results from the University of Michigan's June consumer confidence survey are out. The headline index unexpectedly fell to 82.7 from May's 84.5 reading. The economic conditions sub-index fell to 92.1 from 98.0. the economic outlook sub-index, on the other hand, increased to 76.7 from 75.8.
