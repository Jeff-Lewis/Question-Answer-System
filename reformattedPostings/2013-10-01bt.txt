ISMISM's monthly report on the American manufacturing sector is out. The headline index from the report unexpectedly rose to 56.2 from August's 55.7 reading. Economists predicted the index would fall to 55.0. Any number above 50 indicates expansion while a sub-50 reading indicates contraction, so today's report suggests that the pace of expansion in American manufacturing accelerated last month. The production sub-index advanced to 62.6 from 62.4, but the new orders sub-index fell to 60.5 from 63.2. The employment sub-index jumped to 55.4 from 53.3.
