Beck Diefenbach, ReutersApple today announced that it would be issuing bonds in order to pay for its new shareholder dividend and share buyback program. Credit rating agency Standard &amp; Poors gave the new Apple bonds a 'AA+' rating, outlook stable. That's the same rating S&amp;P has on U.S. government debt, which it downgraded from AAA in July 2011. Right now, there are only four AAA rated companies in the United States: Johnson &amp; Johnson, ADP, Exxon Mobil, and Microsoft.
