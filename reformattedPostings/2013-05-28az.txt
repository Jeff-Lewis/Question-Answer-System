APFormer U.S. Congressman Anthony Weiner has already cut the deficit in the Democratic primary race for New York City Mayor in more than half, according to a new Marist College poll released Tuesday. The poll finds that Weiner trails City Council Speaker Christine Quinn by 5 percentage points. Quinn gets 24 percent of the vote among Democratic primary voters, compared with 19 percent who say they will back Weiner. That is a 6-point swing in the past month for Weiner, after a mid-April Marist poll taken amid rumors of his candidacy found him trailing, 26-15. The poll contains both good and bad news for Weiner.
