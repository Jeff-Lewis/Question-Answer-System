A Swiss referendum on Sunday rejected a move to cap executive pay at 12 times the wage of a firm's lowest earner, according to provisional results aired on local television. The measure had been expected to fail, but the debate has tapped into a vein of discontent among Swiss voters who in March backed rules to rein in golden handshakes, in the wake of high-profile payments to top bosses.
