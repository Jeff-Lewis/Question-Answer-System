Hectorir via FlickrOnce bankers reach a certain age and want to be true bosses in their own right, they often ask themselves — should I get my CFA or my MBA? It's a great question. While the MBA — the Masters in Business Administration — offers variety and camaraderie, the CFA — Chartered Financial Analyst — offers precision and rigor. The MBA means you take a few years and go to school (part or full time) and the CFA is a 3-level test that requires years of solitary study (and sometimes solitary failure).
