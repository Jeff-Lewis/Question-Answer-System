He laid out four pieces of evidence for where his fear comes from: However, Summers is quick to point out that these four data points do not mean that we have entered a period of secular stagnation. They simply are reasons to be concerned. "Today, secular stagnation should be viewed as a contingency to be insured against – not a fate to which we ought to be resigned," he writes.
