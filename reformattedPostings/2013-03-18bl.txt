Earlier this month, we learned that the national unemployment rate unexpectedly fell to 7.7 percent in February. State-level data comes on a lag.  However, the new January state unemployment report confirms that the jobs market continues to get incrementally better. In a post on his Calculated Risk blog, Bill McBride notes that there are now no states with double-digit unemployment rates.  This is the first time that has happened since 2008.
