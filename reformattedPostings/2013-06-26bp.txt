We're a bit taken aback by the newly released revisions to Q1 GDP. GDP growth was slashed to 1.8% from a previous reading of 2.4%. Personal consumption growth was cut to 2.6% from 3.4%. For now, markets are taking it in stride and barely budging. Dow futures are up 60 points. S&amp;P 500 futures are up 9 points. The U.S. 10-year Treasury yield is sitting at 2.52%. It's worth reiterating that this data reflects a period that end in March, which was nearly three months ago.
