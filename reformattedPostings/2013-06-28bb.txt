Traders and bond market watchers are talking more and more about 1994, a year when interest rates exploded higher completely obliterating bond investors. The event also came with sell-off in stocks.  But that eventually turned into a buying opportunity. For months, the strategists at Deutsche Bank have been pushing this thesis as hawkish commentary from the Federal Reserve have sent Treasury rates higher.
