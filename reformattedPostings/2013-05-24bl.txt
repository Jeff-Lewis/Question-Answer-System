boeingmedia.comUPDATE: Durable goods orders data are out. Total orders expanded 3.3% in April after plunging a revised-up 5.9% in March. Economists predicted a smaller, 1.5% rise. Orders excluding transportation equipment rose 1.3% after contracting a revised down 1.7% in March. Economists predicted only a 0.5% gain. Nondefense capital goods orders excluding aircraft and parts (a.k.a. "core capex") rose 1.3% after expanding 0.9% in March. There, economists were only expecting a 0.5% gain.
