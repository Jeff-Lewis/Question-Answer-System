Flickr/kryptoHEADS UP: The world's biggest economies are releasing their September manufacturing PMI reports. And this is our scorecard. All of developed and emerging Asia reflect improvement and acceleration, which is encouraging considering heightened currency volatility in the region. Even Australia reported that its manufacturing sector expanded for the first time since June 2011. So far, the only area really showing cracks is Europe, where the numbers in Spain, Italy, Germany, and Greece deteriorated. Later this morning, we'll get numbers from the U.S. where all signs point to growth, but decelerating growth.  Also, if the U.S. government shuts down as expected, the available economic information will be limited, making these PMI report something to be more scrutinized. PMI At the beginning of each month, Markit, HSBC, RBC, JP Morgan, and several other major data gathering institutions publish the latest local readings of the manufacturing purchasing managers index (PMI) for countries around the world.
