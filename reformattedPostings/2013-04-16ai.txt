Americans' sentiment toward gold as an investment has been on the decline.  But according to a new Gallup poll, gold was nevertheless perceived as the best investment by 24% of Americans. That's more than those who prefer stocks or bonds. Gallup's survey was conducted from April 4-7, which was before the recent gold price collapse. Here's the chart: Gallup And here's a demographic breakdown of the responses: Gallup What's most striking here is the male-female breakdown: while 29% of the former believe gold is safest, just 19% of the latter do.
