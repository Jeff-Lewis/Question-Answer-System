Reuters/Gary CameronConstruction spending beat expectations rising 0.6% in July. Economists polled by Bloomberg were looking a 0.4% month-over-month rise. Meanwhile, June's number was revised up to reflect construction spending stayed flat, compared with an initial print that showed a 0.6% fall. Overall residential spending was up 0.5% on the month,a nd 16.8% on the year. Meanwhile, public safety spending was down 8.1% on the month, and 14.1% on th year.  Investors watch this number because it offers insights into the housing market and into homebuilder stocks.
