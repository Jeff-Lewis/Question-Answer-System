Via Goldman Sachs chief U.S. equity strategist David Kostin: "Large-cap core mutual funds have slightly outperformed the S&amp;P 500 year-to-date, returning 20.4% vs. the market at 19.8%. Hedge funds have continued to lag returning only 3.5%; however long/short equity funds have returned 11.8%." Kostin says those mutual funds are overweight names like Microsoft, Google, Johnson &amp; Johnson, Wells Fargo, and Procter &amp; Gamble; whereas they are underweight names like Apple, Berkshire Hathaway B shares, Pfizer, Exxon Mobil, and IBM.
