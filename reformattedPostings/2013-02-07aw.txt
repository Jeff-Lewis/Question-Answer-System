Shutterstock.comThere's no better evidence of the growing chasm between America's rich and poor than state tax rates.  On average, the nation's lowest-income residents pay tax rates that are twice as high as the wealthiest 1 percent of taxpayers, according to a new report by The Institute on Taxation and Economic Policy.  That includes state, income, property and sales taxes, of which the rich pay 5.6 percent of their income and the bottom 20 percent of earners pay 11.1 percent each year.
