Chinese property stocks are up after the follow-up measures announced by local governments ended up being less strict than markets had expected. The Shanghai Stock Exchange Property Index (SHPROP) is up 2.4 percent. A month ago, when China first announced a 20 percent capital gains tax on home sales, the SHPROP had tumbled nearly 9 percent.
