Orin Zebest on FlickrApril consumer prices data are out. The headline index of prices fell 0.4% in April from the previous month, more than the 0.3% decline predicted by economists. That marks the first back-to-back decline in monthly prices since late 2008. Excluding food and energy, prices rose 0.1%. Economists were looking for a 0.2% increase. On a year-over-year basis, prices rose 1.1% in April, missing expectations for a 1.3% advance.
