Today is the 50th anniversary of the March on Washington. We've made a lot of gains in improving racial equality since then. But a chart from Pew shows that that only goes so far, showing poverty rates by race since 1963. After four decades of gains for blacks, we are now moving backwards (that is, upwards). Today, 28% of African-Americans are below the poverty line, compared with just 10% for whites.
