Monday was another very ugly session in Japan. The Nikkei was down 3.7%, hitting a 1-month low. Nikkei.com As WSJ notes, the index is now down 17% from its recent high, which was just over a week and a half ago, meaning that from a technical standpoint, the index is on the verge of a "bear market." Although even if it's not in one already, this really is a stunningly swift crash. Japan had been the hottest market in the world, thanks to "Abenomics" the fiscal and monetary stimulus scheme proposed by its new Prime Minister. For awhile it was a can't lose investment.
