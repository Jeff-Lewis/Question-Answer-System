Ever since his Tweet about Portuguese debt haircuts apparently caused the country's bond yields to spike, Bill Gross and his giant PIMCO bond fund's Twitter feed has been a big must-follow. This afternoon, Gross offered two year-end Tweet-thoughts about what's moving markets these days.  His answer: artifice.  That is, there have been few fundamentals — "real growth and investment" — to support recent returns. Rather, he says, an "artificial" prosperity has been created via central banks that is similar to the "artificial" prosperity created by Bitcoin holders.   Check it out: Gross:Part 1of 2: We live not in a new gilded age but a bitcoin age where artificial money (from central banks) creates temporary prosperity Gross: Part 2 of 2: Real growth and investment must be the focal point.
