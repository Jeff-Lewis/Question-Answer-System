U.S. crude oil production is on pace to hit 7.3 million barrels a day by the end of this year, according to the EIA. That would put it above total imports, which will be hovering around 7 million barrels a day by December. The last time production outpaced imports was 1995, according to the EIA. And by 2014, production-imports gap could rise to as much as 2 billion barrels.  Here's the chart: EIA The reason, of course, is the shale boom.
