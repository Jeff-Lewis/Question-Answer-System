Flickr / MyTudutDecember consumer credit data is out. Total consumer credit in the United States expanded 6.3 percent at an annualized rate – or $14.6 billion – in December, ahead of expectations of a $14 billion expansion. Revolving credit – credit cards and the like – fell 5.1 percent at an annualized rate to $849.8 billion in December. Non-revolving credit – auto loans and the like – increased 11.4 percent at an annualized rate to $1.93 trillion. The rate of expansion in total consumer credit moderated slightly from November, when credit expanded $15.9 billion.
