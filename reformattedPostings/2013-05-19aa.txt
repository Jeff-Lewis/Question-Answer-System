In the latest Bedtime with BTIG note, strategist Dan Greenhaus passes along this chart of various markets and how they've performed year-to-date. BTIG One thing that really stands out is how bad the performance has been from the BRICs. The two worst on here are Brazil and Russia. China and India are barely in positive territory. Overall emerging markets are down. The winners are the real old-timers: U.K., Japan, and of course the U.S., with Europe closely behind. A big part of the story this year has been the weakness in commodity prices, which is toxic for countries like Brazil, Mexico, and Russia.
