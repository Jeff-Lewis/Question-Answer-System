Not only was the 152,000 level of private sector job creation below estimates and “whisper numbers,” but the U.S. once again relied on low-wage jobs (generally 1/3rd of total jobs in the economy) for 57% of total job creation.   These jobs pay about $15.80/hour, whereas the other 2/3rds of jobs in the economy pay over $27.00/hour.   The economy is not creating jobs that enable workers to hold their own relative to the cost of living.  The detail is to the right (click to enlarge).
