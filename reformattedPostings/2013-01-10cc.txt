Shares of Herbalife are up more than 3 percent as executives from the multi-level marketing company that sells nutrition products rebut hedge fund manager Bill Ackman's short thesis.  The stock has been trading in a range of $39.18 to $40.99. Ackman, who runs Pershing Square Capital Management, is shorting 20 million shares of Herbalife.  He believes the company is a pyramid scheme and has a price target of zero.  Since confirming his short, the stock is off 6%.  It had been off more than 38%.
