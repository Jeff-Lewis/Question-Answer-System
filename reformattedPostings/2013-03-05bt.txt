REUTERS/Robert GalbraithThe IBD/TIPP Economic Optimism Index fell 10.8 percent in March to 42.2, down from 47.3 in February. A reading below 50 indicates net pessimism. "Americans across-the-board think that the economic outlook is grim," said Raghavan Mayur, president of TIPP. "The big slide in our economic outlook sub-index perhaps signals a turning point and an impending entry into a recession. This month sixty percent believe that the economy is in a recession." The index has three key components and all of them decline in March. Consumers have been hit by higher taxes, and the "cynical manipulation and exaggeration of the impacts of the sequester in Washington".
