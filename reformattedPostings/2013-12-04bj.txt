New home sales surged 25.4% month-over-month to an annualized pace of 444,000 in October. While this seems like a huge jump, it is similar to rates of sales seen in Q1 (449,000) and Q2 (443,000) of 2013, before the lagged effect of higher mortgage rates, wrote Michael Gapen of Barclays. Two key things prompted the surge we saw in October. Diggle writes that the new home sales data shows that higher mortgage rates "will not derail the housing recovery."
