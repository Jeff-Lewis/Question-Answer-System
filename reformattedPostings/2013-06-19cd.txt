The growing pile of bearishness on China continues. Last night, Chinese stocks hit a new 6-month low, as concerns rise about growth, debt, acute financial market stress, a property bubble, and everything else. Today, HSBC has significantly slashed Chinese growth. The bank's chief economist Stephen King tweeted the news: HSBC cuts China growth forecasts to 7.4% in '13 &amp; '14 (from 8.2% and 8.4%) as Beijing focuses on supply reforms instead of demand stimulus.
