Stocks continue to sit near five-year highs thanks to corporate profits rebounding from the recession and surging to all-time highs. Meanwhile, the U.S. economic recovery continues to be anemic.  And the biggest symptoms of that are the weak labor market and low wages. These two stories go hand-in-hand because part of the corporate profit rebound has been driven by lower costs in the form of workforce and wage reduction.
